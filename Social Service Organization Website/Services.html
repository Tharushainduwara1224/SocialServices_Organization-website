Sri Lanka has made rapid progress in poverty alleviation. Extreme poverty - less than two percent of the population - is already rare. But a large percentage of Sri Lankans live just above the official poverty line and are at risk of falling out again. The government has declared 2022 as the Year of Poverty Eradication. The end of the conflict in 2009 led to a boom in the construction and service sectors. Inequality in the country increased markedly between 2019/20 and 2012/13. Sri Lanka is the only country in the region where inequality as measured by the Gini Index has increased.
